Problem Statement

The problem with not having a wellness app is that individuals lack an accessible and convenient tool to effectively track, monitor, and improve their physical and mental health. This results in a lack of self-awareness, poor health management, and a hindered ability to make meaningful lifestyle changes. Without the ability to track and monitor health data, set and track goals, receive personalized recommendations, and access community support, individuals may struggle to prioritize their well-being and achieve optimal health.

My Role

I was the sole senior UI/UX engineer assigned to this project. My design role included UX design, visual design, branding and identity. My deliverables included user interviews, user persona, user flows, sketching, wire-framing, and prototyping.

Problem Discovery

Overcoming the Hurdles of Success in the
Wellness App Industry

Companies operating in the wellness app market face several challenges in order to succeed. One of the major challenges is related to data security and privacy concerns. Ensuring the protection of user data and maintaining compliance with privacy regulations is essential in order to build user trust and prevent data breaches. Another challenge is competition in the crowded wellness app market. With many similar apps available, it can be difficult to stand out and differentiate from the competition. In addition to these challenges, companies must also ensure that the app is user-friendly and accessible to attract and retain users. This involves creating a seamless user experience that is easy to navigate and intuitive to use, which can be challenging in an increasingly crowded market.

User Research

Persona

Persona creation is important because it helps businesses and organizations better understand their target audience or customer base. By creating fictional characters that represent the different types of customers or users that they are trying to reach, businesses can gain insight into their customers' needs, behaviors, and preferences. This understanding can then be used to inform marketing strategies, product development, and overall business decisions.

Setting the Design Direction

Wireframes

To determine the experience's general framework, I used a top-down strategy. I created a ton of concepts for the layout of the user interface (UI), functional and data parts, and interaction behaviors through sketching and storyboarding. Our vision began wide and gradually turned into something concrete. The app's anatomy, interactions, and high-level design language started to come together.
